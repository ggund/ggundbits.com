GGundBits is a collection of **hands‑on cloud engineering guides** — the kind of write‑ups I wish existed when you are on a deadline and need something that actually works.

I’m Gaurav Gundal, a **DevOps / cloud engineer** with over 16 years in software and infrastructure, currently working at Amazon Web Services. Over the years I’ve moved from C++ and SQL in banking and BI, through large‑scale automation and VMware/vRA, to Kubernetes, AWS, and platform engineering.

I focus on:

- **AWS and Kubernetes**: EKS, networking, identity, and operations.
- **Enterprise integrations**: Platforms like ServiceNow and how they meet cloud‑native patterns.
- **Infrastructure as Code**: Preferably CDK and other tools that make repeatability the default.

Every post is backed by a **public GitHub repository** so you can:

- See the full implementation, not just snippets.
- Fork and adapt the patterns to your environment.
- Reproduce the architecture from scratch when you come back months later.

If you’d like to connect, you can:

- Follow the code on **GitHub**: [github.com/ggund](https://github.com/ggund)
